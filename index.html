Our Mission

When the world said "some students just can't learn Hebrew," we asked: "Why not?"

Malkah was born from a simple observation: students with ADHD and language-based learning differences were being left behind in yeshiva education. The system had written them off. We refused to accept that.

By breaking down the silos between special education, neuroscience, occupational therapy, physical therapy, and speech-language pathology, we designed a curriculum that teaches Hebrew the way the brain actually learns: multisensory, movement-based, and deeply engaging for every type of learner. While designed for diverse learners, we’ve found this method helps every student thrive.

What started as one-on-one tutoring has grown into a full curriculum now being implemented at yeshivot for kindergarten and first grade, and we are just getting started.

Our Approach

What Makes Malkah Different

Multisensory by Design

Every lesson engages multiple senses simultaneously — visual, auditory, tactile, and kinesthetic — so every learning style is reached.

Rooted in Neuroscience

Our methodology draws from the latest research in how the brain acquires language, combined with proven strategies from OT, PT, and speech therapy.

Inclusive from the Start

Malkah was not retrofitted for inclusion — it was built for it. Students with ADHD and learning differences are not an afterthought; they are our starting point.

De-Siloed Education

We brought together experts from special education, neuroscience, OT, PT, and speech to build something none of them could have created alone.
